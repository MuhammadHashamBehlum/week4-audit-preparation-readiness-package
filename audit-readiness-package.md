## Audit Process

### Stage 1 – Documentation Review

The first stage of an ISO/IEC 27001 audit tends to focus on reviewing the organization's Information Security Management System (ISMS) documentation. The auditors are able to verify that the policies, procedures, risk assessments, and even the supporting records are established, as well as aligned with ISO/IEC 27001 requirements.

### Stage 2 – Implementation Review

The second stage is able to evaluate how effectively the documented controls operate in practice. The auditors review operational evidence, interview personnel, observe business processes, and are able to clearly verify that the security controls are consistently implemented.

## Sample Audit Findings

| Finding | Corrective Action |
|----------|-------------------|
| Privileged access reviews are not consistently documented. | Perform documented quarterly privileged access reviews and retain evidence. |
| Cloud governance documentation requires improvement. | Develop formal cloud governance procedures and maintain supplier assessment records. |
| Multi-factor authentication is not enabled for all business-critical users. | Expand MFA deployment across critical business applications and review implementation regularly. |
